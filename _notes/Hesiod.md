**Hesiod** (c. 8th century BCE) was an ancient Greek poet, thought to have been active around the same time as [[Homer]]. He is the author of *[[Theogony]]*, which tells the origins of the gods, and *[[Works and Days]]*, a poem that describes the five Ages of Man.

Unlike Homer, Hesiod's poems contain some references to his own life, especially in *Works and Days*. Thus, he is considered to be one single, real person, and one of the first authors in the Western tradition to understand himself as an individual persona.
